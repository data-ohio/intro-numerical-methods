Theorem 2.3.3.

Suppose \(A\) is a square matrix. The following are all equivalent:
  1. The equation \(A\xb = \bb\) has 0 or \(\infty\) many solutions depending on \(\bb\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗
  2. \(\det(A) = 0\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗
  3. \(A\) does not have an inverse.
    🔗
    🔗
  4. The equation \(A\xb = \mathbf{0}\) has solutions other than \(\xb = \mathbf{0}\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗
  5. The columns of \(A\) are linearly dependent as vectors.
    🔗
    🔗
  6. The rows of \(A\) are linearly dependent.
    🔗
    🔗
