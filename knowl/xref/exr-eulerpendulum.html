Exercise 2.

The equation of motion of a damped, unforced pendulum is
\begin{equation*} \ddot{\theta}+ \frac{\gamma}{m}\dot{\theta}+ \frac{g}{\ell}\sin \theta = 0\text{.} \end{equation*}
The total energy of the pendulum is \(E = m \ell^{2} \dot{\theta}^{2}/2 + m g \ell (1-\cos(\theta))\text{.}\) Suppose a pendulum has \(m = 2\text{,}\) \(g = 9.81\text{,}\) \(l = 3\text{,}\) and coefficient of friction \(\gamma = 0.5\text{,}\) all in SI units. Write a well-commented script program that uses the Modified Euler’s method with \(h = 0.01\) to simulate the movement of the pendulum from a starting position of \((\theta(0),\dot{\theta}(0)) = (.9 \pi, 0)\) until the energy falls below 0.01 Joules (use a while loop). Record the steps in matrices Y and T and plot the motion. Turn in your program and plot.
